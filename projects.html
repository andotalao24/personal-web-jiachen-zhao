Improving Fairness of Large Language Models
June 2023 – Oct 2023 · supervised by Prof. Mengye Ren and Prof. James Zou
  • Working on understanding the effects of supervised finetuning on LLMs for debiasing from the view of continual learning.
Large Language Models for Clinical Applications
Feb 2023 – Feb 2024· supervised by Prof. Hong Yu
  • Working on automatic Chain-of-Thought generation for clinical QA to resolve shortage of annotations.
  • Working on improving zero-shot QA performance by adding medical jargon to prompts.
Few-shot Parsing for Biomedical Texts
Sep 2022 – Feb 2024 · supervised by Prof. Andrew McCallum
  • Focused on few-shot constituency parsing. Worked on knowledge distillation from LLMs to a smaller applicable language model (i.e., T5).
  • Worked on understanding the performance difference between student and teacher in knowledge distillation, and on more effective KD methods to handle noise in teacher labels.
Patella Segmentation for Early Detection of Osteoarthritis
June 2021 – Feb 2022 · supervised by Prof. Hao Chen
  • Increased the robustness and data-efficiency of CNNs by leveraging Statistical Shape Models (SSM) for patella segmentation.
  • Implemented automated sampling and image registration to generate SSM. Proposed a metric for predicting whether CNN or SSM performs better with no access to ground truth. Proposed a fuzzy nearest-neighbor framework to integrate prior anatomical knowledge into CNN-based segmentation.
  • Increased the Dice coefficient of mainstream CNN frameworks by ~1.7% on blind tests; models reached comparable performance with half the training data.
Event Detection Without Triggers
Sep 2021 – Jan 2022 · supervised by Prof. Haiqin Yang
  • Worked on low-resource event detection without annotated trigger words. Reformulated the classification task as reading comprehension.
  • Proposed an event derangement module to resolve imbalanced learning; performance on minority events improved. Two patents under registration.
  • Trained on real large-scale data from Datastory and deployed in the company's product for events detection on social media data, greatly reducing annotation cost.
